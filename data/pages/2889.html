flxpygame

An attempt to port Flixel to run on top of pygame (or at the least, save others some of the effort of doing so).

Current targets (and testing platform):

  • Flixel - 2.55
  • Python - 2.7.4
  • PyGame - 1.91release

Why?

I believe that making programming accessible is significantly more important than making programming... Well, much of anything else, really. I come from the school of thought that says gamifying helps a lot in many circumstances, and also from the school of thought that says we need to enable people to work as they choose whenever possible. So... Yeah...

There is also a valid nod here to the fact that I want a quick way to prototype virtually everything I do, really like Flixel, and need some Python code on display to prove that I know Python because it's the only language I claim to know to any degree and have nothing that impressive (or non-Mark Guzdial) to show for it. The timing is right as well as the need for work to occupy my mind as a distraction from other things present in my life.

On top of this, I'd like to understand Flixel itself better. Despite giving input at various points, maintaining a branch for the sake of testing for the community and my own work's progress, and generally wishing to help out... I don't understand Flixel itself well. So this is an effort to cure that, in its own right.

Why PyGame?

Well... Why not? PyGame has the benefit of being made for game development and simple application development, cross platform and (generally) stable. So I can't think of a much better library, personally, in python. Bindings may find their way in later - or be written by those interested in doing so - for various pieces of Python that could be considered useful. (Blender's formats, etc.)
