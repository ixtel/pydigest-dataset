I'm a third-year graduate student at the Institute for Astronomy at the University of Hawaii working with Eric Gaidos and Dan Huber on finding and characterizing giant planets orbiting giant stars. I'm also working with Stuart Jefferies on understanding the subsurface structure of the Sun. Previously, I worked with Andrew Howard on a new statistical method to describe stellar noise in exoplanet mass measurements. Check out this news article to learn more.

I'm interested in better understanding the relationship between planets and their host stars in the context of planet formation and evolution, and the search for extraterrestrial life. In my free time, I enjoy making music, attempting to surf, and teaching scientific programming skills in Python.
